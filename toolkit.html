Opening Script - Setting the Right Tone

You:

"Hi, my name is [Your Name] and I'm calling about my bill, account number [Number]. I received a bill for $[Amount] which seems significantly higher than standard rates. I'd like to discuss a fair resolution."

Tip: Stay calm and professional. You're not asking for charity - you're negotiating inflated charges.
Them:

"I can look that up for you. What seems to be the issue?"

You:

"I've researched standard pricing for these services. For example, you've charged $847 for a facility fee, but Medicare pays around $200 for the same service. I have several similar overcharges I'd like to review."

Power Phrase: "I'm prepared to pay a fair amount today if we can reach an agreement."

Handling Common Pushback

Objection: "These are our standard rates."
Your Response:

"I understand these are your listed rates, but they're 400% higher than what insurance companies pay. I'm asking for the same fair pricing that Blue Cross would receive. Surely we can find a middle ground that works for both of us."

Objection: "We can't negotiate prices."
Your Response:

"I appreciate your position, but I know hospitals routinely adjust bills. Could you please connect me with someone in financial counseling or a supervisor who has the authority to discuss this? I'm trying to avoid filing complaints with the state insurance commissioner."

Objection: "You'll need to apply for financial aid."
Your Response:

"I'm not seeking charity care. I'm disputing the accuracy of these charges. A $43 charge for one ibuprofen is not accurate pricing - it's an error that needs correction. Can we please focus on adjusting these specific overcharges?"

Manager Escalation Script

You:

"Thank you for taking my call. I've been trying to resolve billing errors on account [Number]. Your team member was helpful but indicated they don't have authority to make adjustments. As a supervisor, I hope you can help me resolve this today."

Tip: Managers can do more than they initially admit. Be persistent but respectful.
Continue:

"Here's my situation: The total bill is $[Amount]. Based on Medicare rates and fair market pricing, a reasonable charge would be around $[Fair Amount]. I'm prepared to pay $[Offer] today to settle this account in full. This saves us both time and your hospital avoids collection costs."

Power Phrase: "What do I need to do to pay $[Offer] today and close this account?"
If needed, add leverage: "I've documented all overcharges and I'm prepared to file complaints with the state attorney general, the insurance commissioner, and post detailed reviews online. I'd prefer we settle this between us."

Closing the Deal

When they make an offer:

"I appreciate you working with me on this. So you're offering to settle the full balance for $[Their Offer]. Can you confirm this will close the account completely with no additional bills?"
